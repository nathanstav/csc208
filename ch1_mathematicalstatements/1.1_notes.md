## 1.1.2 Atomic and Molecular Statements
- A **statement** is declarative sentence that is either true or false
- If a statement cannot be divided into smaller statements, it is **atomic**.
    - i.e... <br>
    - Telephone numbers in the USA have 10 digits. <br>
    - The moon is made of cheese. <br>
    - 42 is a perfect square.
    - etc...

- If a statement CAN be divided into smaller statements, it is considered **molecular**.
- $3 + x = 12$ is not a statement, as it contains a variable. Since we don't know what $x$ is equal to, the statement may or may not be true, and right now it is neither true nor false.
    - To make this a statement, we could do a couple things to it:
    - "$3 + x = 12$ where $x = 9$" (substitution)
    - "For all values of $x$, $3 + x = 12$" (quantification)
