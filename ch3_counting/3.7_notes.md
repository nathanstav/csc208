### Additional Exercises
5. How many people do you need to have in a room to have a 50% chance that at least two people share the same birthday (day of the year)?
   - The chance that two people have **different** birthdays is $\frac{364}{365}$ in fraction form.
   - This means that $1 - (\frac{364}{365})^x \geq 0.5$, where $x$ is the amount of pairs of people
   - Using a Desmos regression, we get that $x$ is equal to at least $253$
   - Now, we know that the number of pairs of people, or $\frac{n(n+1)}{2} = 253$, where $n$ is the number of people in the group.
   -  Using another Desmos regression, we find that $n$, or the people in the group, is equal to $22$.
