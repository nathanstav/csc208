### Additional Exercises
5. How many people do you need to have in a room to have a 50% chance that at least two people share the same birthday (day of the year)?
   - The chance that two people have **different** birthdays is $\frac{364}{365}$ in fraction form.
   - This means that $1 - (\frac{364}{365})^x \geq 0.5$, where $x$ is the amount of pairs of people
   - Using a Desmos regression (or just some algebra), we get that $x$ is equal to at least $253$
   - Now, we know that the number of possible pairs of people is equal to $253$, so $253 = \binom{n}{2}$, where $n$ is the amount of people.
   - This means that $\frac{n!}{2!(n-2)!} = 253$
   - Using another Desmos regression (or some algebra), we can find that $n$ is equal to $23$
   - This means we need a room with at least $23$ people for there to be a 50% chance that two or more people share the same birthday.
