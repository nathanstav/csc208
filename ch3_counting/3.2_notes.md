### What are Outcomes?

- Counting problems are usually asked in terms of an **event** that can result in different **outcomes**, and the outcomes are counted.
- Counting problems could also be described in the way of just counting the number of elements in a *set* of *outcomes*.
- If you just make a numbered list, you could say the bow ties that I have:
  1. Purple
  2. Green
  3. Striped
  4. Paisley
- Since the largest number in the numbered list is $4$, I have 4 bow ties. 
- Let's say I also had a list of seven pairs of novelty socks. If I wanted to combine the two lists to make a new list with length 11:
  - For example, I could first list the four bow ties and then keep counting (starting with 5 for the first pair of socks) until you have all 11 items.
- This is a new set of outcomes with 11 elements, meaning we have 11 choices of items (if we were choosing only one item)
- If we wanted to wear both a bow tie and a pair of socks, we need to combine them in a different way:
  - The new set of outcomes would need to have $28$ ($7 * 4$) elements
  - All 28 elements have both a bow tie and a pair of socks
- The first of these types of combinations is combining the *sets* of outcomes
- The second is combining the *outcomes* in the sets

### Sum and Product Principles
- If an event $A$ results in $m$ outcomes, and event $B$ results in $n$ disjoint (unrelated) outcomes, then the event "$A$ or $B$" results in $m + n$ outcomes.
  - If they were not disjoint, for example a deck of 52 cards with 26 red cards and 12 face cards, this formula does not work because there are $6$ cards which are both red and face cards.
- Examples:
  - How many 2-letter sequences of letters begin with A or B?
    - $52 (26 + 26)$
  - How many $10$-bit strings of weight $6$ start with either $11$ or $00$
    - $\binom{10}{6} = 210$
    - If the first 2 are $11$, then the remaining 8 bits must have 4 more 1's:
      - $\binom{8}{4} = 70$
    - If the first 2 are $00$, then the remaining 8 bits must have 6 more 1's:
      - $\binom{8}{6} = 28$
    - $70 + 28 = 98$
  - How many $10$-bit strings of weight $6$ have at least one $1$ in their first three bits?
    - there are $7$ strings with length $10$ and weight $6$ that start with $000$:
      - $\binom{7}{6} = 7$
    - $x + 7 = 210$
    - $x = 203$

- If an event $A$ can occur in $m$ ways, and each possibility for $A$ allows for exactly $n$ ways for event $B$, then the event "$A$ and $B$" can result in $m \cdot n$ ways.
  - Examples:
    - How many 2-letter words start with one of the 5 vowels?
      - If event $A$ is the vowel that starts the word, which can happen in $5$ ways, and event $B$ is the second letter which can happen in $26$ ways
      - $5 \cdot 26 = 130$
    - How many lattice paths from $(0, 0)$ to $(10, 10)$ pass through the point $(4, 7)$?
      - Paths from $(0, 0)$ to $(4, 7)$ is $\binom{11}{4} = 330$
      - Paths from $(4, 7)$ to $(10, 10)$ is $\binom{9}{3} = 84$
      - We must combine these by *multiplying*, as any path from between the first 2 points could connect to any 2 from the second 2 points
      - $330 \cdot 84 = 27720$

### Practice Problems
1. Your wardrobe has 2 shirts, 4 pairs, and 18 bow ties. How many outfits can you make?
   - $2 \cdot 4 \cdot 18 = 144$ outfits
2. For your college interview, you must wear a tie. You own 2 regular (boring) ties and 6 (cool) bow ties.
   - How many choices do you have for your neck-wear?
     - $2 + 6 = 8$
   - You should probably wear both a regular tie and a bow tie. How many choices do you have now?
     - $2 \cdot 6 = 12$
   - You have 6 shirts, 5 skirts, 7 pants, and 8 dresses. Either wear a shirt with a skirt or pants, or just a dress. How many outfits to choose from?
     - $6 \cdot 5 + 6 \cdot 7 + 8 = 80$
3. Hexadecimal (0, 1, ..., 9, A, B, C, D, E, F):
   - How many 4-digit hexadecimals with first digit E or F?
     - $2 * 16 * 16 * 16 = 8192$
   - How many 6-digit hexadecimals starting with A-F and ending with 0-9?
     - $6 * 16 * 16 * 16 * 16 * 10 = 3,932,160$
   - How many 5-digit hexadecimals start with a letter (A-F) or end with a numeral (0-9) (or both)?
     - $(6 * 16 * 16 * 16 * 16) + (10 * 16 * 16 * 16 * 16) - (6 * 10 * 16 * 16 * 16) = 802816$

9. How many subsets of {0, 1, ..., 9} have cardinality 8 or more?
   - Cardinality 8: $\binom{10}{8} = 45$
   - Cardinality 9: $\binom{10}{9} = 10$
   - Cardinality 10: $\binom{10}{10} = 1$
   - $45 + 10 + 1 = 56$
10. How many shortest lattice paths start at $(1, 1)$ and
    - end at $(12, 12)$
      - $12 - 1 = 11$, $11 + 11 = 22$ total choices
      - $\binom{22}{11} = 705432$
    - end at $(12, 12)$ and pass through $(6, 7)$
      - $12 - 6 = 6$, $12 - 7 = 5$, $5 + 6 = 11$
      - $\binom{11}{6} = 462$
      - $6 - 1 = 5$, $7 - 1 = 6$, $5 + 6 = 11$
      - $\binom{11}{5} = 462$
      - $462 \cdot 462 = 213,444$
    - end at $(12, 12)$ and avoid $(6, 7)$
      - $705,432 - 213,444 = 491,988$
